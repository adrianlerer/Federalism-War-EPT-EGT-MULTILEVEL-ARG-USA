## 3. THEORETICAL ADVANCEMENTS IN CURRENT PAPER

### 3.1 ESS Conditionality (Major Refinement)

**SSRN Paper Claim** (p. 6):
> "Populism constitutes an Evolutionarily Stable Strategy if: (1) E(P,P) ≥ E(L,P) for all L; (2) If E(L,P) = E(P,P), then E(P,L) > E(L,L). Both conditions hold in our calibrated model."

**Current Paper Refinement** (Section 1, Finding 2):
> "ESS stability is **conditional on regime type**: clientelism is stable under Establishment regimes (Kirchner, Sánchez) but **unstable** under Counter-Establishment regimes (Milei, Trump)."

**Evidence**: Milei dismantled 300+ puntero units in 6 months → **ESS can be disrupted**

**Implication**: The 216:1 memetic fitness advantage applies **within** regime, not **across** regime transitions.

- **Resolution**: SSRN prediction was **directionally correct** (hybrid unstable) but **outcome surprised** (resolved toward Type B, not populist reversion). Add discussion of Counter-Establishment exceptionalism.

**Issue 3: Memetic Fitness vs. EI**
- **SSRN**: Measures **transmission fitness** (how easily memes spread)
- **Current**: Measures **extraction efficiency** (how much resources captured)
- **Resolution**: These are **orthogonal dimensions**. A policy can have high memetic fitness (spreads easily) but low EI (doesn't extract much). Example: Milei's "gerentes de la pobreza" framing has high memetic fitness but **reduces** EI.
