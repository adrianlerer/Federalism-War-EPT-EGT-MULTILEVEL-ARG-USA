### C. Public Choice Analysis

**Classic Structure**:
- **Concentrated Benefits**: 
  - Spanish exporters (CAF, Talgo): €1.5B contracts = **high per-firm benefit**
  - Foreign governments: €260–365M subsidy (vs market rates) = **high per-country benefit**
  
- **Dispersed Costs**:
  - Spanish taxpayers: €75/taxpayer = **low per-capita cost** (invisible)
  - Spanish passengers: Accident risk from alleged maintenance underfunding = **low perceived probability** (pre-Adamuz crash)

**Buchanan & Tullock Prediction**: Concentrated benefits > dispersed costs → **over-provision** of foreign credits relative to social optimum.

**Empirical Confirmation**: €1.5B foreign credits (2019–2025) while domestic maintenance allegedly underfunded = **over-provision** of export subsidies, **under-provision** of domestic infrastructure.

**Institutional Phenotypes**:
1. **FIEM** (export credit fund): Government agency that channels taxpayer money to exporters via foreign credits

## VII. POLICY IMPLICATIONS: How to Stop Transborder Clientelism

### A. Problem Diagnosis

**Root Cause**: Export credit agencies (FIEM, US Ex-Im Bank, Sinosure) = **off-budget fiscal extraction**
- **Mechanism**: Credits appear as "investments" (not spending) in budget accounting → escape fiscal rules
- **Reality**: Credits at 0.5% interest (vs 3–5% market) = **implicit subsidy** (~€260–365M for Spain €1.5B credits)
- **Electoral Benefit**: Concentrated (exporters) > Dispersed Cost (taxpayers) → over-provision

---

### B. Proposed Reforms

#### 1. Require Full Subsidy Disclosure in Budget
**Problem**: FIEM credits reported at face value (€754.3M) not subsidy value (~€150M NPV).

**Solution**: **Mark-to-Market Accounting** for export credits
- Report **net present value of subsidy** (difference between concessional rate 0.5% and market rate 3–5%)
- Include subsidy in **annual budget deficit** (not off-budget)
- **Precedent**: US Congressional Budget Office (CBO) requires subsidy cost estimates for Ex-Im Bank loans

**Effect**: Makes transborder clientelism **visible** to voters → increases political cost.

---

#### 2. Prohibit Tied Aid (Spanish Firm Conditionality)
**Problem**: FIEM credits require Spanish supplier (CAF, Talgo) → guarantees exporter benefit → political clientelism.

**Solution**: **Untie Credits** (allow competitive bidding from any country)
- If Spain provides €754.3M credit to Morocco → Morocco can choose **best supplier** (CAF, Alstom, Siemens, CRRC)
- **Precedent**: OECD Recommendation on Untying Official Development Assistance (2001) → but FIEM is export credit, not ODA, so exempt

**Effect**: Removes concentrated benefits to Spanish exporters → reduces electoral incentive for transborder clientelism.

**Counter-Argument**: Spanish jobs lost to foreign competitors (CAF, Talgo lay off workers).  
**Rebuttal**: If Spanish firms competitive → will win contracts anyway. If not → subsidy is **wasting taxpayer money** on uncompetitive firms.

---

#### 3. Independent Cost-Benefit Analysis for Export Credits
**Problem**: FIEM decisions made by **Consejo de Ministros** (PSOE cabinet) → no independent oversight.

**Solution**: **AIReF** (Autoridad Independiente de Responsabilidad Fiscal) must approve all export credits >€500M
- **CBA Requirements**: 
  - Domestic jobs created (direct + indirect)
  - Subsidy cost (NPV)
  - Alternative uses of funds (domestic infrastructure, healthcare, education)
  - Geopolitical benefits (quantified if possible)
- **Publication**: AIReF analysis published 60 days before Consejo de Ministros vote

**Effect**: Forces government to justify transborder clientelism with **quantitative evidence** → increases accountability.

---

#### 4. Sunset Clause for Export Credits to Non-OECD Countries
**Problem**: Morocco, Egypt, Uzbekistan = middle-income countries (not least developed) → less justification for concessional credits.

**Solution**: **Automatic Expiration** for export credits to non-OECD countries after 5 years
- FIEM can provide credits to Morocco (2025) → expires 2030 → must be **re-authorized by Parliament** (not just Consejo de Ministros)
- **Precedent**: US Trade Promotion Authority (TPA) = expires every 5 years, requires Congressional renewal

**Effect**: Forces periodic re-evaluation → prevents indefinite transborder clientelism.
