## Fryer's Framework Applied to Federal-State Wars

### Fryer's MLK Analysis (Signaling Equilibrium)
- **Players:** Black protesters (hidden types: peaceful reformers vs violent) + White establishment (hidden types: segregationists vs uneasy moderates)
- **Strategy:** Nonviolence is **costly signaling** → separates types → moderates update beliefs → repression becomes expensive
- **Equilibrium shift:** Bad equilibrium (violence → easy repression) → Good equilibrium (nonviolence → reform)
- **Empirical evidence:** Wasow (2020): nonviolent protests → +1.6-2.5pp Democratic vote; violent protests → +5pp Republican vote; Collins & Margo: riots → -10-20% property values, lasting damage

## The Deep Game: Establishment vs Counter-Establishment

### Fryer's Insight Generalized
Fryer shows MLK **constructed a coalition** (black protesters + uneasy white moderates) to defeat segregationists **without economic resources**. The tool was **costly signaling** (nonviolence).

**Our Federal-State Wars analog:**
- **Establishment:** Tech (CA), Finance (NY), Oil (TX), Agriculture (Midwest) — control economic resources
- **Counter-Establishment:** Political elites who **cannot compete economically** (Newsom, Walz, Insfrán, La Matanza Peronists)
- **Tool for Counter-Establishment:** Construct dependent base (immigrants, social program beneficiaries, census inflation) → extract federal resources → build political power

**Key parallel:**
- MLK used nonviolence as costly signal to **separate types** (peaceful reformers vs violent)
- Counter-Establishment uses **clientelist extraction** to **separate loyalists** (dependent beneficiaries vs independent citizens)

## Revised EGT Framework: Three-Level Game with Power-Base Construction

### Level 1: Individual (Immigrant, Beneficiary, Firm)
- **Strategy:** Accept dependency (benefits, tax breaks, subsidies) from subnational elite
- **Payoff:** Material benefit + risk (deportation, audit, tax enforcement)

### Level 2: Subnational Elite (Governor, Intendente, Provincial Political Class)
- **Strategy:** Construct dependent base (sanctuary policies, ghost NGOs, census inflation) → extract federal resources → build political power
- **Payoff:** Federal resources extracted - cost of blocking federal enforcement
- **Constraint:** Cannot compete with Establishment on economic resources alone

### Level 3: Federal Government (President, ICE, ANSES, INDEC)
- **Strategy:** Enforce (deportations, audits, census corrections) vs Acquiesce (tolerate extraction)
- **Payoff:** National political coalition - cost of enforcement

### ESS Conditions (Revised)
**Counter-Establishment extraction is ESS when:**
1. Federal enforcement cost > resources extracted (C_fed > R_extracted)
2. Dependent base is large enough to deliver electoral power (N_dependent > threshold)
3. Economic Establishment cannot easily co-opt Counter-Establishment (resource gap is large)

**Federal acquiescence is ESS when:**
1. Enforcement triggers violent resistance (bad equilibrium à la Fryer)
2. Moderate voters update beliefs against enforcement (à la Wasow 2020)
3. Counter-Establishment's dependent base is large + politically mobilized

## Policy Implications (Revised)

### For Federal Government
1. **Distinguish nonviolent non-cooperation from violent resistance:** CA-style non-cooperation is **optimal for Counter-Establishment** (à la MLK); MN-style violence is **self-defeating** (à la 1960s riots). Federal response should differ:
   - **Nonviolent (CA):** Negotiate federal-state compact (e.g., CA pays for immigrant benefits, no federal funds; in exchange, no ICE raids)
   - **Violent (MN):** Swift enforcement (Insurrection Act) is cheap (Fryer's bad equilibrium)

2. **Reform Census to exclude non-citizens from apportionment:** Removes incentive for sanctuary states to maintain dependent base

3. **Audit social programs for fictitious beneficiaries:** Argentina's Formosa model (ghost NGOs) is replicable in USA (e.g., welfare fraud)

### For Subnational Governments
1. **Adopt CA strategy (nonviolent non-cooperation), NOT MN strategy (violent resistance):** Fryer/Wasow show violence is self-defeating

2. **If you lack economic resources, construct dependent base via legal means:** Tax competition (San Luis), vote-trading (Catamarca), NOT census fraud or ghost beneficiaries

### For Voters/Moderates
1. **Distinguish humanitarian from clientelist:** True humanitarian policy = accept enforcement + provide benefits; clientelist policy = block enforcement + extract federal funds

2. **Demand transparency:** Census data, NGO registries, social program audits should be public
