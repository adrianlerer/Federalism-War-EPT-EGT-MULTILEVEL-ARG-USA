## Background: Insurrection Act (10 U.S.C. Â§Â§ 251-255)

### Legal Framework

The Insurrection Act authorizes the President to deploy US military domestically in three scenarios:

**Â§ 251 (State Request)**: President may deploy military if state legislature (or governor if legislature cannot convene) requests assistance suppressing insurrection.

**Â§ 252 (Federal Law Enforcement)**: President may deploy military to enforce federal laws when:
1. Unlawful obstructions, combinations, or assemblages
2. Make it impracticable to enforce federal law by ordinary judicial proceedings

**Â§ 253 (Civil Rights Enforcement)**: President may deploy military to suppress insurrection that:
1. Opposes or obstructs federal law execution
2. Impedes state/federal authority

### Historical Precedents

**Recent invocations**:
- **1992**: Bush I (LA Riots) â€” invoked Â§ 252 after Governor requested assistance
- **1957**: Eisenhower (Little Rock) â€” invoked Â§ 253 to enforce desegregation orders

2. **Not invoked** for policy disagreements or non-cooperation

**Trump I attempt** (2020): Trump threatened to invoke Insurrection Act during George Floyd protests but **did not follow through** after Pentagon/DOD resistance.

### Legal Constraints

**Posse Comitatus Act** (18 U.S.C. Â§ 1385): Prohibits using military for domestic law enforcement **except** when authorized by Constitution or statute (Insurrection Act is statutory exception).
