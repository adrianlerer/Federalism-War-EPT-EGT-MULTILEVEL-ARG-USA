## Executive Summary

In January 2025, Minnesota Governor Tim Walz publicly announced state-level resistance to renewed federal Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) deportation operations under the Trump II administration. This case represents an **escalation** beyond passive non-cooperation (California SB 54 model) to **active resistance**, including:
- Public denunciations of ICE raids as "illegal" and "unconstitutional"
- State police non-cooperation directives
- Legal challenges to federal warrants
- Political mobilization of immigrant communities and advocacy groups

**EPT Analysis**: Minnesota constructed a political/legal "defense network" to protect its immigrant population (~300,000 undocumented + refugees) while signaling progressive credentials to national/presidential political audiences (Walz was 2024 VP candidate).

**EGT Analysis**: Active resistance becomes an ESS when:
1. Electoral returns (mobilizing immigrant/progressive voters) > federal punishment risk
2. Legal ambiguity (commandeering doctrine) provides "safe harbor" for state defiance
3. National political stage rewards visible "resistance" posturing

**Key Metrics**:
- **Undocumented population**: ~95,000 (2024 estimate, Pew Research Center)
- **Refugee/immigrant population**: ~200,000+ (Somalia, Hmong, Latinx communities)
- **Federal funds at risk**: ~USD 15B/year (Medicaid, SNAP, education, transportation)
- **Estimated electoral impact**: 2-3 percentage points (2026 gubernatorial race, 2028 presidential)
- **EI Score**: 0.60 (High non-cooperation, escalated resistance)

## The Minnesota Strategy (January 2025-Present)

### Phase 1: ICE Operations Announcement (January 2025)
**Federal context**:
- **Trump II administration** (inaugurated January 20, 2025) announces renewed interior enforcement
- **ICE directives**: Target "criminal aliens" + expand enforcement to "all removable aliens" (reversal of Biden-era prioritization)
- **Minnesota targeting**: ICE announces operations in Minneapolis/St. Paul metro (Somali community high concentration)

**Trigger event**: ICE raids in Minneapolis (January 25-28, 2025)
- **Reported arrests**: ~150 individuals (mix of undocumented + refugees with deportation orders)
- **Media coverage**: Viral videos of ICE agents at mosques, schools â†’ public outcry

### Phase 2: Walz Response (January 28, 2025)
**Public statement** (press conference):
> "These raids are illegal, unconstitutional, and un-American. Minnesota will not be complicit in tearing apart families. I am directing state law enforcement to refuse cooperation with ICE operations that violate due process and human rights."

**Policy directives**:
1. **Minnesota State Patrol**: Refuse to provide ICE with logistical support (e.g., roadblocks, information sharing)
2. **State agencies**: Prohibit ICE access to non-public areas of state buildings without judicial warrants
3. **Legal defense**: Minnesota Attorney General (Keith Ellison) to challenge ICE warrants in federal court

**Justification (stated)**:
- "Federal government is violating constitutional rights of Minnesota residents"
- "We will not allow our state to become a police state"

**Justification (EPT analysis)**:
- **Electoral signal**: Mobilize progressive base (immigrant communities, urban liberals) for 2026 gubernatorial race
- **National profile**: Position Walz as "resistance leader" for potential 2028 presidential run
- **Resource protection**: Preserve immigrant labor force (~USD 5B annual GDP contribution to Minnesota economy)

### Phase 3: Institutional Reinforcement (February 2025)
**Mechanism**: Build political/legal infrastructure to sustain resistance.

#### a) Legislative Proposals
- **HF 123** (Minnesota Values Act, introduced February 2025):
  - Mirrors California SB 54 (prohibit local cooperation with ICE)
  - **Status**: Pending in DFL-controlled House (likely passage) + divided Senate (uncertain)

#### b) Local Ordinances
- **Minneapolis City Council** (February 10, 2025): Passes resolution declaring Minneapolis a "Sanctuary City"
  - Prohibits Minneapolis Police from cooperating with ICE detainers
  - Provides legal defense fund (USD 1M) for arrested immigrants

#### c) Legal Challenges
- **AG Keith Ellison** (February 15, 2025): Files lawsuit in U.S. District Court (D. Minnesota)
  - **Claims**: ICE operations violate 4th Amendment (unlawful search/seizure) + Printz commandeering doctrine
  - **Requests**: Injunction against ICE raids + declaratory judgment that Minnesota cannot be compelled to cooperate

### Phase 4: Federal Pushback (February-March 2025)
**Federal response**:
1. **DHS Secretary statement** (February 20, 2025):
   - "Governor Walz is obstructing federal law enforcement and endangering public safety. We will consider all available enforcement mechanisms, including withholding federal funds."

2. **DOJ actions**:
   - Threatens criminal charges against state officials for "harboring" undocumented immigrants (8 U.S.C. Â§ 1324)
   - **Legal basis**: Questionable (no precedent for prosecuting state officials for non-cooperation)

3. **Fund withholding threats**:
   - DHS announces review of Minnesota's federal grants (~USD 15B/year)
   - **Target**: Transportation, law enforcement, refugee resettlement programs

**Minnesota counter-response**:
- **Walz**: "Bring it on. We will see them in court."
- **Political mobilization**: Rallies in Minneapolis (20,000+ attendees, February 22, 2025)
- **Legal defense**: ACLU, immigrant advocacy groups join AG Ellison's lawsuit as amici

**Current status** (March 2025 as of scenario): Legal standoff; no funds actually withheld yet; ICE operations continue but with reduced state cooperation.
