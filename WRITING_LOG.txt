- **Added**: Section 9.3.5 "The Objective Trap: Why Explicit Goals Block Reform" (~1,400 words)
- **Source**: Stanley, K. O., & Lehman, J. (2015). *Why Greatness Cannot Be Planned: The Myth of the Objective*. Springer.
- **Core Argument**: Explicit objectives create deceptive local optima, trigger counter-adaptations, and freeze stepping stones
- **Applied to Clientelism**:
  - Argentina: Macri's explicit anti-corruption campaign → provincial resistance, EI −0.05 (insufficient)
  - Spain: PP denounced caciques → replicated networks (local optimum)
  - Minnesota: Trump explicit deportation objective → $200M state resistance
  - Contrast: Obama/Biden (2009-2024) built refugee network via stepping stones (humanitarian programs) without declaring demographic objective
- **Implication**: Open-ended reform (fiscal transparency, judicial independence, campaign finance) might reduce clientelism as side effect better than explicit "end clientelism" campaigns
- **Connection to Framework**: ESS (Section 9.3) + deceptive objectives (Stanley & Lehman) = lethal combination making reform fail
- **Future Research**: Test whether transparency reforms (stepping stones) reduce EI more than anti-corruption commissions (explicit objectives)
